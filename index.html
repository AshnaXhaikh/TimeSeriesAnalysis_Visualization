📖 What is Time Series Analysis?

📊 Definition: Time series data is a sequence of observations collected at regular time intervals.
🎯 Main Goal: Understand patterns, make predictions, and support decision-making.
🏭 Applications: Finance, Healthcare, Retail, Weather, Social Media

📈 Understanding Trend

📈 What is Trend? Long-term direction — increasing, decreasing, or stable.
🎯 Types: Upward 📈, Downward 📉, Sideways ➡️, Exponential 🚀

🔄 Understanding Seasonality

🔄 What is Seasonality? Regular, predictable patterns at fixed intervals.
📅 Examples: Ice cream sales ↑ summer, Retail ↑ December

📊 Types of Moving Averages

📊 SMA

✅ Best for: Basic trends
📈 EMA
More weight to recent
✅ Best for: Fast reaction
⚖️ WMA
Custom linear weights
✅ Best for: Custom strategies

🔗 Autocorrelation (ACF) - Complete Guide

🔗 What is Autocorrelation? Measures similarity between a series and its lagged version.
Formula: Corr(yₜ, yₜ₋ₖ)
📊 ACF Plot Anatomy: • ■ Bars = Correlation • --- Blue lines = 95% significance threshold
📖 How to Read ACF: • Bars inside blue band = Not significant • Slow decay = Non-stationary • Spikes at regular intervals = Seasonality

🔄 Differencing - Complete Guide

🔄 What is Differencing?
Differencing is a transformation that subtracts the previous observation from the current observation to remove trend and make the time series stationary.
📐 First Order Differencing: y'ₜ = yₜ - yₜ₋₁
📐 Second Order Differencing: y''ₜ = y'ₜ - y'ₜ₋₁
📐 Seasonal Differencing: y'ₜ = yₜ - yₜ₋ₘ (where m = seasonal period)
✅ Why Use Differencing?
• Removes trend and seasonality
• Makes the series stationary
• Required for ARIMA models
• Stabilizes the mean over time
⚠️ When NOT to use Differencing:
• Data is already stationary (over-differencing adds noise)
• You want to preserve the original scale for interpretation
• The series has a deterministic trend (use detrending instead)
📊 First Order Differencing
Removes linear trend
Formula: yₜ - yₜ₋₁
📉 Result: Series fluctuates around constant mean
📈 Second Order Differencing
Removes quadratic trend
Formula: (yₜ - yₜ₋₁) - (yₜ₋₁ - yₜ₋₂)
📉 Result: Very smooth, but can amplify noise
🔄 Seasonal Differencing
Removes seasonal patterns
Formula: yₜ - yₜ₋ₘ (m = season period)
📉 Result: Removes repeating patterns
💡 Key Insight: After differencing, check if the series is stationary by looking at the ACF plot. A stationary series will have ACF bars that quickly drop to zero (no slow decay).

🔧 What is Decomposition? Breaking down into: Trend + Seasonality + Residual

⚡ Stationarity & ADF Test

⚡ Stationarity: Statistical properties (mean, variance) constant over time.
📊 ADF Test: Null hypothesis = series is non-stationary. p-value ≤ 0.05 → Reject null → Stationary!
